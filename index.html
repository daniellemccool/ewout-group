Prevalance

How frequently is this happening?

1997

Thirteen articles in [the contemporary group] contained 20 continuous predictive variables (38% of continuous predictive variables in group A) that were treated only as categoric variables.
Del Priore G, Zandieh P, Lee MJ. Treatment of continuous data as categoric variables in obstetrics and gynecology. Obstet Gynecol 1997;89: 351-4.

2010

A total of 29 (50%) articles reported only an analysis of the categorised form of the continuous exposure...
Turner, Elizabeth L., Joanna E. Dobson, and Stuart J. Pocock. "Categorisation of continuous risk factors in epidemiological publications: a survey of current practice." Epidemiologic Perspectives & Innovations 7.1 (2010): 9.

The article

The Good

Why it's done

  • Simplifies the statistical analysis
  • Helps with presentation
  • Non-normal data/non-linear relationships

The Bad

What are the possible consequences?

  • Loss of information -> loss of power
  • Increase false positive rate
  • Underestimation of variability within a group
  • You will account for less confounding

The Ugly

Type-I error rates for logistic models with two to five categories of cont. var using the quantiles (A) method, β2 = 2 and sample size = 2000.

Barnwell‐Ménard, Jean‐Louis, Qing Li, and Alan A Cohen. "Effects of Categorization Method, Regression Type, and Variable Distribution on the Inflation of Type‐I Error Rate when Categorizing a Confounding Variable." Statistics in Medicine, 34.6 (2015): 936-949.
